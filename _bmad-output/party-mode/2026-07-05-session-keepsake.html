Session log
Exhibit 1 — The Register

142,236 names, zero industries

Mary, Business Analyst"The columns are only Organisation Name, Town/City, County, Type & Rating, Route. No industry, no size, no website. 'F‑Secure (UK) Limited' — I happen to know that's cybersecurity. The register doesn't."

Why it mattered: the real government file, downloaded live mid-conversation, turned "build a company classifier" into a precise, boundaried problem instead of a vague one.

Exhibit 2 — The Downsize

Qdrant, LangGraph, and Playwright leave the room

Winston, System Architect"That's a Series A engineering team's stack for what is, structurally, one person's job search."

Why it mattered: the first honest resizing — from a twelve-agent platform to a lookup table and a linear pipeline.

Exhibit 3 — The Confession

"This is custom, building it for my personal use"

Victor, Innovation Strategist"Then you're not launching a product, you're building yourself a weapon. Different bar entirely."

Why it mattered: every architecture decision after this point got measured against one user's evening hours, not a market.

Exhibit 4 — The Reversal

Job-first beats company-first

Amelia, Senior Engineer"You only ever enrich a company the moment it has a live posting that matches you. You never touch the other 121,800 sponsors who don't."

Why it mattered: flipped the whole discovery direction — and revealed a job-search connector already sitting in the room, unused.

Exhibit 5 — The Redacted Client

Agency name given. Employer: ████████

Mary, Business Analyst"If your system checks the sponsor register against the agency's name because that's the only name given, you'll get a verdict that has nothing to do with who'd actually employ you."

Why it mattered: turned "can't verify" into an honest, first-class answer — and handed the cold-DM instinct a second justification.

Exhibit 6 — The Coach

"Like a personal AI mentor"

Maya, Design Thinking Maestro"A status report says '3 follow-ups pending.' A coach says 'you've been quiet for two days — everything okay?'"

Why it mattered: reframed the entire tool around one daily screen instead of a pile of dashboards nobody opens.

Exhibit 7 — The Deadline, Un-sugarcoated

December 31st was never the real date

Winston, System Architect"UK hiring slows from mid-December. Treat the real deadline as early-to-mid December for a signed offer — that's 22 effective weeks, not 26."

Why it mattered: the room's one hard truth, delivered on request, with the calendar math to back it.

Verdict
Filed & Closed

Converged V1 — the core loop

  • Cleaned sponsor register as a lookup table — no enrichment until a job actually needs it
  • Paste-in job intake, honest three-case sponsor check (direct / agency-redacted / user-supplied)
  • SOC-code salary threshold check, so a licensed sponsor can still be correctly ruled out
  • Match score → tailored resume & cover letter, GitHub-evidence backed
  • Channel-aware cold DM drafts, run off one narrative core
  • Application tracker, keyed on company + role, with day 3/7/14 reminders
  • Goal / Roadmap Planner, pulled into the core loop — deadline pressure was real
